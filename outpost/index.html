VPS Two-Campus Strategy · April 2026

Two large centres. One system. Nothing like it in Kochi.

Transform VPS Lakeshore into a purely referral-driven specialist hospital — tertiary and quaternary cases only, anchoring Kerala's most complex care: transplant, cardiac surgery, CAR-T, and neurosurgery. Build VPS Kakkanad as a full-scale multispecialty hospital on the SmartCity land parcel — akin to how Medeor Abu Dhabi operates as a high-quality, accessible, community-facing centre that captures everyday volume and feeds complex cases upward. Together they serve Kochi's fastest-growing urban population — young IT professionals, NRI families, and the 140,000-strong Infopark–SmartCity workforce — through a managed referral loop no competitor in Kerala can replicate.

Recommendation — proceed to full due diligence
⚠ Draft — for internal discussion only. All financial projections, market sizing, catchment estimates, and competitive assessments in this document are high-level indicative figures based on publicly available data and internal assumptions. They have not been independently validated and should not be relied upon for investment decisions without formal external validation by qualified advisors.

The strategic direction is clear. No hospital group in Kochi operates two large, purpose-differentiated campuses. Aster Medcity is a single quaternary centre trying to be everything. Amrita is anchored in Edappally. Rajagiri is tertiary. VPS has a rare and time-limited opportunity to build the only genuine two-hospital system in Kerala — VPS Lakeshore as the specialist referral hub, and VPS Kakkanad modelled on what Medeor has achieved in Abu Dhabi: a premium, accessible, community-facing multispecialty centre that captures high everyday volume and channels complex cases upward.

VPS Lakeshore becomes Kerala's most focused specialist and referral hospital. Kakkanad becomes the premium multispecialty centre for a young, growing, insured urban population that currently has no quality option in its backyard. Together they build a system that no competitor can replicate without land, capital, and a decade of time.

Recommended next steps — due diligence and evaluation:

1
Appoint an independent financial and market advisor — commission a formal feasibility study covering demand validation, competitive benchmarking, financial modelling (DCF, IRR, sensitivity analysis), and regulatory review. Firms with relevant track record: Grant Thornton, KPMG Healthcare, EY Health Sciences, or a specialist healthcare investment bank.
2
Land and legal due diligence — conduct title verification, SEZ compliance review, FAR and zoning confirmation, and environmental clearance assessment for both Option 1 (5.85 Ac) and Option 2 (6.17 Ac) parcels. Engage a Kerala-based real estate legal firm alongside SmartCity's development team.
3
Operator and clinical model validation — engage hospital planning consultants (HKS, EDAC, or equivalent) to develop a concept brief, department programme, and bed mix validation for a 400-bed multispecialty facility on 6.17 Ac. Confirm phased build viability and infrastructure requirements.
4
Corporate TPA pipeline development — begin informal conversations with HR Directors at top 10 Infopark corporates (TCS, Wipro, HCL, IBM, Cognizant) to gauge willingness to switch or add VPS as a panel hospital. This runs in parallel with due diligence and costs nothing.
5
Board sanction and capital planning — present the validated feasibility study and clinical concept brief to the VPS Healthcare board for investment approval. Explore financing structures: internal accruals, project finance, strategic partnership, or a combination.
